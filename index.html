In the city that never sleeps, does love advertising has its opening hours?

By Joanna Yen

Craigslist is an early online advertising platform where people post jobs and sell furniture. This digital town has a missed connection corner where people take a second chance at love.

In June, just shy of 200 New Yorkers found themselves in a similar situation, posting a missed connection on Craigslist. These posts blended the excitement of potential connection in a city of eight million with a tinge of regret. They captured fleeting moments, a shared glance, a brief chat with a stranger on the subway, in a park, or during a bike ride.

These fleeting romancesare not bound by time or placefor Manhattanites. They happenon the subway, in parks,orbars at any given moment. In a city that never sleeps, lonely hearts in Manhattan never seem to rest.

Brooklyn comes in second in Craigslist love advertising. These solitary souls are specific about neighborhoods. The frequent mentions of Williamsburg and Park Slope are a trying effort to narrow down their search, praying for a response.

Despite their daily hustle and bustle, people in Queens still pay attention to the passersby. Their fleeing moments usually occur during their daily routine, shopping, or commuting.

Lonely hearts in the Bronx often described brief visual connections or short conversations in transit or on the street. These writings documented the borough's pride and tight-knit community with interactions among locals.

Staten Island's missed opportunities revolved around the ferry, parks, and local businesses. They are primarily recurring sightings or fleeting romances with extended conversations.
